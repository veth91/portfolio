A developer in denial

I have always been a “computer guy.” My friends know who to come to when their mac blinks the grey-screen-of-death. I even worked as a desktop support tech in college, but I always thought developing was too hard for me. Still, I taught myself Python with a free online book for my senior thesis: using a climate model written completely in Python (I graduated with a B.S. in Geosciences). After college, I let my Python knowledge slip away but continued to surround myself with tech. I worked in the GeoSciences field, most enthralled when science and tech met. I digitally mapped the web of roads in America’s National Forests, built a Wordpress site for a non-profit dedicated to protecting a Montana watershed, and taught a class to middle schoolers on basic web development. In 2016 my life changed when I co-founded FeatureMe. My partner handled the product fulfillment, while I handled the tech. Well, "handle" may be too weak of a verb. I lived the tech. I spent hours solving problems, the time whizzing by, implementing my endless list of features to make product fulfillment just that much smoother. But I was using Squarespace and felt very limited. After a year of FeatureMe, I decided to step down from the operation to seriously pursue what I discovered was my true passion — web development. From a mixture of Udemy courses, online courses, friends, and endless articles, I learned what I know today. I encourage you to browse my portfolio and check out my GitHub to see the skills I’ve cultivated. I’m no longer a developer in denial. Now, I’m a developer. ~Brandon
